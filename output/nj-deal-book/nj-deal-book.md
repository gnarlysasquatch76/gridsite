## Executive Summary

This deal book presents 10 high-potential data center development sites in New Jersey, identified through a systematic analysis of stranded power capacity, retired generation facilities, and vacant industrial parcels near high-voltage transmission infrastructure.

**The Thesis:** When large industrial consumers close — manufacturing plants, refineries, distribution centers — they leave behind utility infrastructure sized for 10-100+ MW loads. This infrastructure can take 3-5 years and $50M+ to build new. By identifying these stranded capacity sites before they're widely marketed, we can acquire sites at industrial land prices and deliver them to data center developers at a significant premium.

**Why New Jersey:**
- Top-tier fiber connectivity (97% broadband coverage, dense metro fiber)
- Adjacent to NYC financial and cloud markets
- NJ Next AI incentive program ($250M/project)
- PSEG and JCP&L territory with available transmission capacity
- Competitive utility rates vs. Northern Virginia
- Growing hyperscaler demand pushing out of Ashburn/NoVA

**How These Sites Were Selected:**
1. NJ WARN Act filings (plant closures, 100+ employees, 36 months)
2. Industrial closure news scan (manufacturing, chemical, distribution)
3. PSEG/JCP&L load loss and PJM queue withdrawal research
4. EIA retired/retiring power plant database
5. Vacant industrial parcels within 2 miles of 138kV+ substations
6. Scored on: Power Availability (30%), Transmission (20%), Acquirability (20%), Transportation (15%), Site Readiness (15%)

### Methodology

Sites were identified through three data pipelines:
1. **WARN Act Analysis:** NJ Department of Labor WARN filings filtered for plant closures with 100+ employees in heavy industrial sectors (36-month window)
2. **News & Utility Research:** Anthropic Claude API with web search to identify industrial closures, PSEG/JCP&L load loss, and PJM queue withdrawals
3. **Infrastructure Analysis:** EIA-860 retired plant database and HIFLD substation data filtered for NJ with 138kV+ transmission
